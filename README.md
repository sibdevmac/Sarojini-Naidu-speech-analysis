# 🌸 Speech Analysis of Sarojini Naidu  

## 🕊️ Death Anniversary Tribute – 2nd March 2026  

On the death anniversary of Sarojini Naidu, a prominent nationalist and feminist leader, this project analyzes one of her speeches to understand its emotional tone, rhetorical framework, and leadership qualities using Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques.

🔗 **Speech Source:** https://speakingwhilefemale.co/womens-lives-naidu/  

---

## 📌 Project Objective  

The objective of this project is to:

- Perform **Sentiment Analysis**
- Examine **Rhetorical Framing Terms**
- Analyze **Pronoun Usage Patterns**
- Interpret leadership and nationalist themes through linguistic evidence

---

## 📊 Analysis & Insights  

### 1️⃣ Sentiment Polarity  

The sentiment analysis graph reveals:

- ✅ Positive Words: **37**
- ❌ Negative Words: **5**

#### 🔎 Interpretation:
The strong presence of positive words highlights:

- Inspirational tone  
- Optimistic leadership  
- Emotional resilience  
- Compassion-driven nationalism  

This demonstrates statesman-like qualities rooted in encouragement rather than criticism.

---

### 2️⃣ Rhetorical Framing Terms  

The rhetorical framing graph indicates frequent usage of words related to:

- Service  
- Duty  
- Responsibility  
- Nation  
- Sacrifice  

#### 🔎 Interpretation:
Her speech reflects a **Servant Leadership Model**, emphasizing responsibility toward the nation and collective progress.  

This reinforces:

- Patriotic commitment  
- Ethical leadership  
- National unity  

---

### 3️⃣ Pronoun Usage Analysis  

The pronoun usage graph shows that Naidu used:

- More **collective pronouns** (we / our / us)
- Fewer **directive pronouns** (you / your)

#### 🔎 Interpretation:
This suggests:

- Team-oriented leadership  
- Inclusive rhetoric  
- Democratic spirit  
- Collaborative national vision  

Her linguistic choices indicate belief in shared responsibility rather than autocratic authority.

---

## 🏁 Conclusion  

The analysis concludes that Sarojini Naidu’s speech is overwhelmingly positive and motivational, particularly for women and the broader national community. This study demonstrates how linguistic analysis can uncover leadership traits embedded within historical speeches.
